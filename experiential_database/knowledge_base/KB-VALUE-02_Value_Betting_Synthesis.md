## Core Concepts of Value Betting

Value betting is the practice of only placing a bet when the odds offered are better than the horse's assessed chance of winning. This is also known as seeking "overlays." The core idea is to treat betting like shopping, where the goal is to find bargains.

### True Odds vs. Offered Odds

- **True Odds:** The real probability of an event occurring. In horse racing, this is a subjective assessment made by the handicapper.
- **Offered Odds:** The price offered by the bookmaker or tote.

Successful long-term punting relies on consistently identifying discrepancies between your assessed true odds and the offered odds, and only betting when the offered odds are higher.

### The Importance of Price

- **Any Price a Winner is a Myth:** The idea that any price is good as long as you back a winner is a fallacy. Accepting odds that are lower than a horse's true chance of winning (an "underlay") is a recipe for long-term losses.
- **Economic Value:** The central theme is that betting is about economic value. If the price isn't right, the bet should be avoided, regardless of how likely the horse is to win.

### The Dangers of Short Odds

- **Maiden Gallopers at Short Odds:** Backing inexperienced horses at short odds is generally considered poor value.
- **Champions vs. the Rest:** While a champion horse might justify short odds, most horses do not.

## The Process of Value Betting

1.  **Assess True Odds:** The first step is to handicap the race and assign a probability of winning to each horse. This is a subjective process based on form, class, weight, and other factors.
2.  **Frame a Market:** Convert your assessed probabilities into a "true odds" market.
3.  **Compare with Offered Odds:** Compare your true odds with the real-time market odds.
4.  **Identify Overlays:** Identify horses where the offered odds are significantly higher than your assessed true odds. These are your value bets.
5.  **Discipline and Patience:** The key to successful value betting is the discipline to pass on races where no value is present and the patience to wait for the right opportunities.
