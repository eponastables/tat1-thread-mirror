**Key Characteristics:**

*   **Pear-Shaped Course:** Flemington's unique shape and long, sweeping turns are a defining feature, generally allowing for fair racing where most runners get their chance. [2]
*   **The Straight Six:** The 1200m straight course is a unique feature in Australian racing. It negates barrier draw advantages and becomes a pure test of a horse's speed and stamina. [4]
*   **Long Home Straight:** The 450m home straight is one of the longest in Australia, providing ample opportunity for backmarkers to make up ground, assuming the pace is genuine. This characteristic makes Flemington a true "galloper's track". [3]

## 2. Pace & Running Style Bias

**General Profile:** Flemington is considered one of the fairest tracks in Australia. Its long straight and wide, sweeping turns mean that both on-pace runners and closers can win, depending on the race distance, rail position, and early pace.

**Betfair In-Running Position Analysis (Horses up to $10 SP):**

| Position in Running | Strike Rate (SR%) | Profit on Turnover (POT%) | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **On Lead (1st/2nd)** | 20.00% | +9.40% | [5] |
| **Handy (3rd/4th)** | 19.00% | +4.60% | [5] |
| **Back (7th+)** | 13.50% | -16.60% | [5] |

**Analysis:**

*   The Betfair data shows a clear, positive return for horses racing on or near the lead. While the strike rate for leaders (20%) is not as dominant as at tighter tracks like Moonee Valley, the profitability (+9.40% POT) is significant.
*   Horses settling further back than 4th show a negative return, with those 7th or worse performing very poorly (-16.60% POT). This confirms that even on a fair track like Flemington, being too far back is a significant disadvantage.

**Practical Punting On-Pace Runner Statistics (All Tracks):**

A comprehensive study of over 38,000 races by Practical Punting Monthly confirms the universal advantage of on-pace runners. Key findings applicable to Flemington include:

*   **54.3% of all winners** are positioned in the first four at the 800m mark. [6]
*   **61.5% of all winners** are positioned in the first four at the 400m mark. [6]
*   Backmarkers (9th or worse at the 800m) win only **8.6%** of races. [6]

## 3. Barrier Draw Bias

**RacingZone Barrier Statistics (Since 2017):**

| Distance | Key Barrier Insights | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint (<=1400m)** | Barriers 7 & 8 show a notably high strike rate (10.7% and 16.4% respectively). Inside barriers (1-3) are solid, but there is no distinct disadvantage to drawing wide in straight races. | [7] |
| **Mile (1400m-1800m)** | Barriers 5 & 6 show the highest strike rates (11.6% and 15.4%). The inside gates (1-4) are less favoured in these middle-distance events. | [7] |
| **Middle Distance (1800m-2400m)** | Barrier 3 (13.6%), Barrier 6 (15.0%), and Barrier 9 (13.0%) are the most successful. The inside two barriers have a 0% strike rate in this sample. | [7] |

**Analysis:**

*   **Straight Races (1000m-1200m):** As noted by PuntFormPro, inside barriers are less critical. The ability to run straight and finish strongly is paramount. [4]
*   **Races Around the Bend:** For races with a turn out of the straight, an inside draw is generally preferred to avoid covering extra ground. However, the RacingZone data suggests this is not a simple "inside is best" rule, with middle draws often performing very well.

## 4. Strategic Implications for TDM

1.  **On-Pace Advantage is Real:** Despite its reputation as a fair track, the data confirms a strong statistical and financial advantage for horses racing in the first four. Your model should apply a positive weighting to horses with a high probability of settling in a forward position.
2.  **Backmarkers are a Poor Proposition:** Unless there is a very strong reason to believe the early pace will be exceptionally fast, horses that are habitual backmarkers should be treated with extreme caution. The -16.6% POT for this group is a significant hurdle to overcome.
3.  **The Straight Six is a Specialist Course:** Do not treat a 1200m race down the straight the same as a 1200m race around a bend. Barrier draws are less important, and the ability to sustain a long, uninterrupted sprint is the key attribute.
4.  **Middle Draws Can Be Favourable:** The barrier statistics suggest that being drawn in the middle of the pack (barriers 5-9) is often advantageous, potentially allowing for tactical flexibility to find a good position without being crowded on the rail.
