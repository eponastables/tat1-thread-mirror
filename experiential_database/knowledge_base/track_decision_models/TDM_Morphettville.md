## 1. Track Configuration

| Parameter | Main Track | Parks Track | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Circumference** | 2,339 metres | 1,805 metres | [1] |
| **Home Straight** | 334 metres | 317 metres | [1] |
| **Direction** | Anti-clockwise (Left-handed) | Anti-clockwise (Left-handed) | [1] |

**Key Characteristics:**

*   **Two Tracks in One:** Morphettville has two distinct tracks: the main course and the smaller "Parks" track. It is crucial to know which track is being used on any given day. [1]
*   **Main Track:** A large, spacious track with a long 334m straight. It is considered a very fair track. [1]
*   **Parks Track:** A much tighter circuit with shorter straights and sharper turns. It races very differently to the main track. [1]

---

## 2. Pace & Running Style Bias

**General Profile:** The pace bias at Morphettville is entirely dependent on which track is in use.

*   **Main Track:** Generally considered a fair track where all horses get their chance. The long straight allows backmarkers to run on.
*   **Parks Track:** Has a significant on-pace bias due to the tighter turns and shorter straight. It is difficult to make up ground from the back. [1]

**PuntFormPro Analysis:**

PuntFormPro's guide explicitly states to "Favour On-Pace Runners on the Parks Track" as "Speed and positioning are critical." [1]

**The Great Tip Off Analysis:**

Racing journalist Heath Pope notes that the track "traditionally favours runners on or near the pace." [2]

## 3. Barrier Draw Bias

**RacingZone Barrier Statistics (Since 2017 - Combined Tracks):**

| Distance | Key Barrier Insights | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint (<=1400m)** | Barrier 1 has a high 11.6% strike rate. Barriers 1-6 generally hold an advantage. | [3] |
| **Mile (1400m-1800m)** | Barrier 2 has a massive 20.0% strike rate. Inside draws are a significant advantage. | [3] |
| **Middle Distance (1800m-2400m)** | Barrier 2 (20.8%) and Barrier 5 (15.0%) are the most successful. | [3] |

**Analysis:**

*   **Strong Inside Bias:** The combined statistics show a strong bias towards inside barriers, particularly barrier 2 in mile and middle-distance races.
*   **Parks Track vs Main Track:** The bias towards inside draws is likely to be even more pronounced on the tighter Parks track.

---

## 4. Strategic Implications for TDM

1.  **Two Separate Models:** The TDM for Morphettville must be a dual model, with two distinct sets of weightings: one for the Main Track and one for the Parks Track.
2.  **Parks Track Model:** This model should be similar to the Moonee Valley model, with a strong positive weighting for on-pace runners and inside barriers.
3.  **Main Track Model:** This model should be more balanced, similar to the Flemington model, with a moderate on-pace advantage but still allowing for the possibility of closers winning.
4.  **Barrier 2 is a Hotspot:** The model should reflect the exceptionally high strike rate from barrier 2 in mile and middle-distance races.
