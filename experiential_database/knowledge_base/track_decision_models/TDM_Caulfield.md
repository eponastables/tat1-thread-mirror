| **Direction** | Anti-clockwise (Left-handed) | [1] |

**Key Characteristics:**

*   **Triangular Shape:** Caulfield is often described as a triangular-shaped course with three distinct, tight turns. This is a unique feature among Australian tracks. [1]
*   **Short Home Straight:** The 367m straight is relatively short, placing a premium on being well-positioned before the final turn. It is difficult for backmarkers to make up significant ground. [1]
*   **Banked Turns:** The turns are banked, which can help horses maintain speed through the bends. [1]

## 2. Pace & Running Style Bias

**General Profile:** Caulfield is widely regarded as a track that favours on-pace runners. The tight turns and short straight make it difficult for horses to come from behind.

## 3. Barrier Draw Bias

**RacingZone Barrier Statistics (Since 2017):**

| Distance | Key Barrier Insights | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint (<=1400m)** | Barrier 3 has an exceptionally high 20.4% strike rate. Barriers 1-6 generally hold an advantage. | [3] |
| **Mile (1400m-1800m)** | Barrier 3 (13.0%) and Barrier 6 (16.7%) are very strong. There is a clear advantage to drawing inside. | [3] |
| **Middle Distance (1800m-2400m)** | Barrier 3 (16.7%) and Barrier 5 (15.4%) are the most successful. Again, an inside draw is highly beneficial. | [3] |

**Analysis:**

*   **Strong Inside Bias:** Like Randwick, Caulfield exhibits a very strong bias towards inside barriers across all distances. The high strike rate from barrier 3 is a notable feature.
*   **The 1400m Chute:** The start for 1400m races is in a chute that leads into a turn, making an inside draw particularly important to avoid being caught wide.

## 4. Strategic Implications for TDM

1.  **On-Pace and Inside is the Golden Rule:** The TDM for Caulfield should be built around the core principle of favouring horses with early speed that have drawn an inside barrier. The combination of a short straight and tight turns makes this a powerful combination.
2.  **Barrier 3 is a Hotspot:** The consistently high strike rate from barrier 3 across all distances suggests this is a particularly advantageous starting position. The model should reflect this.
3.  **Wind is a Key Variable:** As noted by Trevor Lawson, wind direction can significantly impact the bias. A strong headwind in the straight can negate the on-pace advantage and bring closers into play. The model should be sensitive to wind data if available.
4.  **Backmarkers Face a Tough Task:** The short straight and tight turns make it very difficult for horses to win from the back. The model should apply a strong negative weighting to habitual backmarkers, even more so than at Flemington or Randwick.
