**Key Characteristics:**

*   **Sweeping Turns:** Rosehill is known for its spacious, sweeping turns, which generally allow for fair racing and give most horses a chance to find a position. [3]
*   **Long Home Straight:** The 408m straight is long enough to allow horses to make up ground from off the pace, but it is not as long as Flemington's, meaning positioning is still crucial. [1]
*   **Fair Track:** Overall, Rosehill is considered a very fair track where the best horse on the day often wins. [3]

## 2. Pace & Running Style Bias

**General Profile:** Rosehill has a reputation for favouring on-pace runners, particularly when the rail is out. The relatively short run to the first turn in many starts makes early speed important.

**Betfair In-Running Position Analysis (Horses up to $10 SP):**

| Position in Running | Strike Rate (SR%) | Profit on Turnover (POT%) | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **On Lead (1st/2nd)** | 24.20% | +15.70% | [4] |
| **Handy (3rd/4th)** | 17.70% | -7.50% | [4] |
| **Back (7th+)** | 14.00% | -15.00% | [4] |

**Analysis:**

*   The data shows a **very strong bias** towards horses leading or sitting second. A 24.2% strike rate and a massive +15.70% Profit on Turnover makes this the single most dominant running style at the track.
*   Unlike Flemington, even being "Handy" (3rd/4th) results in a negative return (-7.50% POT). This suggests that at Rosehill, you need to be in the first pair to have a significant statistical advantage.
*   Backmarkers perform poorly, consistent with most Australian tracks, returning a -15.00% POT.

**PuntFormPro Analysis:**

PuntFormPro's guide explicitly states that Rosehill has a "Strong On-Pace Bias" and that the track "often favours horses that settle near the lead, especially when the rail is out." [3]

## 3. Barrier Draw Bias

**RacingZone Barrier Statistics (Since 2017):**

| Distance | Key Barrier Insights | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint (<=1400m)** | Barrier 2 has a strong 8.6% strike rate. There is a general advantage to drawing inside barriers 1-6, with strike rates declining for wider gates. | [5] |
| **Mile (1400m-1800m)** | Barriers 2 and 3 are strong (10.6% and 13.3% respectively). The advantage of inside draws is less pronounced than in sprints, but still evident. | [5] |
| **Middle Distance (1800m-2400m)** | Barrier 1 has a very high 18.2% strike rate. This is a significant anomaly and suggests a strong advantage for the inside gate in staying races. | [5] |

**Analysis:**

*   **Inside Draws are Key:** Across all distances, there is a clear statistical advantage to drawing an inside barrier at Rosehill. This is particularly true for sprint races and, surprisingly, for middle-distance races from the 1800m+ starts.
*   **1100m & 1200m Starts:** The quick run to the first turn makes an inside draw particularly important. A horse drawn wide without exceptional early speed is likely to be caught wide and lose ground.

---

## 4. Strategic Implications for TDM

1.  **Prioritise Leaders Above All:** The TDM for Rosehill must heavily weight horses with a high probability of leading or sitting second. The +15.70% POT for this group is too significant to ignore. This is the primary pace characteristic of the track.
2.  **Downgrade "Handy" Runners:** Unlike other tracks where being in the first four is sufficient, at Rosehill the data suggests the advantage is concentrated in the first two positions. Horses likely to settle 3rd or 4th should not receive the same positive adjustment as genuine leaders.
3.  **Inside Barriers are a Major Factor:** The model should incorporate a strong positive weighting for inside barriers (1-5), especially in sprint races and races over 1800m+.
4.  **Rail Position is Critical:** As noted by PuntFormPro, the on-pace bias is exacerbated when the rail is moved out. The model should be sensitive to rail position, increasing the weighting for on-pace runners as the rail moves further from the true position.
