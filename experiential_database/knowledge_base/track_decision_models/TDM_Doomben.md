## 2. Pace & Running Style Bias

**General Profile:** Doomben has a well-deserved reputation as a track that strongly favours on-pace runners. The tight turns and short straight make it very difficult to make up ground from the back.

**Betfair In-Running Position Analysis (Horses up to $10 SP):**

| Position in Running | Strike Rate (SR%) | Profit on Turnover (POT%) | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **On Lead (1st/2nd)** | 23.60% | +15.10% | [2] |
| **Handy (3rd/4th)** | 17.70% | -10.00% | [2] |
| **Back (7th+)** | 11.70% | -33.00% | [2] |

**Analysis:**

*   **Very Strong Leader Bias:** The data is clear. A 23.6% strike rate and a +15.10% POT for leaders makes this a very leader-biased track, second only to Moonee Valley in this sample.
*   **Catastrophic for Backmarkers:** The -33.00% POT for backmarkers is the worst of any track in the Betfair analysis. It is exceptionally difficult to win from the rear at Doomben.
*   **"Handy" is Not Enough:** As with Moonee Valley and Rosehill, even racing in 3rd or 4th results in a significant negative return. The advantage is heavily concentrated in the leading pair.

## 3. Barrier Draw Bias

**RacingZone Barrier Statistics (Since 2017):**

| Distance | Key Barrier Insights | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint (<=1400m)** | Barrier 1 has a massive 18.2% strike rate. Barriers 1-5 have a clear and significant advantage. | [3] |
| **Mile (1400m-1800m)** | Barrier 1 (17.8%) and Barrier 3 (13.3%) are very strong. The inside bias is pronounced. | [3] |
| **Middle Distance (1800m-2400m)** | Barrier 10 has a huge 23.1% strike rate, and barrier 12 has 25.0%. This is a major anomaly and suggests that in staying races, a middle-to-wide draw may be advantageous, perhaps to get a clear run away from the rail. | [3] |

**Analysis:**

*   **Extreme Inside Bias in Sprints and Miles:** For races up to 1800m, drawing an inside gate is a huge advantage. The 18.2% strike rate for barrier 1 in sprints is a critical statistic.
*   **The Staying Race Anomaly:** The high strike rate for wide barriers in staying races is counter-intuitive and requires further investigation. It may be that in slowly run staying races, being away from the rail allows horses to build momentum without being cluttered up.

## 4. Strategic Implications for TDM

1.  **A Moonee Valley Clone:** The TDM for Doomben should be very similar to the Moonee Valley model. It must be heavily weighted towards on-pace runners, especially the likely leader.
2.  **Barrier 1 is Key (up to 1800m):** The model must apply a very strong positive weighting for barrier 1 in all races up to 1800m.
3.  **Penalise Backmarkers Heavily:** The model should apply its strongest negative weighting for backmarkers at Doomben. The -33.00% POT is a clear signal that they are a very poor betting proposition.
4.  **Investigate the Staying Race Anomaly:** The model should be cautious about applying a simple inside barrier bias to staying races. The data suggests a different dynamic is at play, and further research into the pace of these specific races is warranted.
