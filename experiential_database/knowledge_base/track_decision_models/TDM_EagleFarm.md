## 1. Track Configuration

| Parameter | Value | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Circumference** | 2,027 metres | [1] |
| **Home Straight** | 434 metres | [1] |
| **Direction** | Clockwise (Right-handed) | [1] |

**Key Characteristics:**

*   **Long, Sweeping Turns:** Eagle Farm is a big, spacious track with long, sweeping turns. This generally allows for fair racing where horses can find their position. [1]
*   **Long Home Straight:** The 434m straight is one of the longest in Queensland, giving closers plenty of time to make up ground. [1]
*   **"Galloper's Track":** Due to its size and long straight, Eagle Farm is often referred to as a "galloper's track," suiting horses with a long stride and strong finishing burst. [2]

## 2. Pace & Running Style Bias

**General Profile:** Eagle Farm is considered a very fair track, similar to Flemington. The long straight means that both on-pace runners and backmarkers can win, depending on the race tempo.

**Betfair In-Running Position Analysis (Horses up to $10 SP):**

*Note: The Betfair analysis does not provide specific data for Eagle Farm. The general Australian metropolitan track data from Practical Punting Monthly will be used as a proxy.*

**PuntFormPro Analysis:**

PuntFormPro's guide notes that the long straight "suits horses with a powerful finish, often overcoming on-pace runners." However, it also notes that when the rail is out, on-pace runners may hold an advantage. [2]

## 3. Barrier Draw Bias

**RacingZone Barrier Statistics (Since 2017):**

| Distance | Key Barrier Insights | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint (<=1400m)** | Barrier 5 has a high 11.8% strike rate. There is no strong bias towards inside or outside barriers in sprints. | [3] |
| **Mile (1400m-1800m)** | Barrier 5 (13.7%) and Barrier 7 (12.7%) are the most successful. Middle draws appear to be favoured. | [3] |
| **Middle Distance (1800m-2400m)** | Barrier 1 has a very high 25.8% strike rate. This is a significant advantage in staying races. | [3] |

**Analysis:**

*   **Fair in Sprints and Miles:** For races up to 1800m, there is no significant barrier draw bias. The long runs to the turns and the spacious nature of the track allow horses to find a position from most gates.
*   **Inside Advantage in Staying Races:** The 25.8% strike rate for barrier 1 in races over 1800m is a major anomaly and suggests a strong advantage for the inside gate in these events.

---

## 4. Strategic Implications for TDM

1.  **A Fair Track Model:** The TDM for Eagle Farm should be the most balanced of all the tracks. It should not have a strong default bias towards any particular running style.
2.  **Focus on Finishing Ability:** The model should place a high value on a horse's finishing burst and its ability to sustain a long run. The 434m straight is where races are won and lost.
3.  **Barrier 1 in Staying Races:** The model must incorporate the significant advantage of drawing barrier 1 in races over 1800m.
4.  **Rail Position is a Key Variable:** The model should be sensitive to rail position. When the rail is out, the weighting for on-pace runners should be increased.
