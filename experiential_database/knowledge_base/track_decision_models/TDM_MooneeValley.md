| **Circumference** | 1,805 metres | [1] |
| **Home Straight** | 173 metres | [1] |
| **Direction** | Anti-clockwise (Left-handed) | [1] |

**Key Characteristics:**

*   **Extremely Short Straight:** At just 173m, Moonee Valley has the shortest home straight of any metropolitan track in Australia. This is the single most important feature of the track. [1]
*   **Tight, Sharp Turns:** The track is a tight, oval shape with sharp, banked turns. This favours agile horses that can accelerate quickly around the bends. [1]
*   **The "Cauldron":** The amphitheatre-like setting creates a unique atmosphere, and the track is known for producing thrilling, tactical races. [1]

## 2. Pace & Running Style Bias

**General Profile:** Moonee Valley is the most leader-biased track in Australia. The short straight provides very little time for backmarkers to make up ground, and horses that lead or race on the pace have an enormous advantage.

**Betfair In-Running Position Analysis (Horses up to $10 SP):**

| Position in Running | Strike Rate (SR%) | Profit on Turnover (POT%) | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **On Lead (1st/2nd)** | 26.30% | +28.00% | [2] |
| **Handy (3rd/4th)** | 17.40% | -9.00% | [2] |
| **Back (7th+)** | 11.90% | -30.80% | [2] |

**Practical Punting "Valley of Bias" Study:**

A detailed study by Practical Punting Monthly in 1999 found an extremely strong bias towards frontrunners, with an RF (Relative Frequency) Factor of 1.98, meaning leaders win almost twice as often as would be expected by chance. [3]

**Analysis:**

*   **Extreme Leader Bias:** The data is unequivocal. A 26.3% strike rate and a massive +28.00% POT for leaders is one of the strongest statistical biases in Australian racing. This is the defining characteristic of Moonee Valley.
*   **Nowhere to Come From:** The -30.80% POT for backmarkers is a catastrophic figure. It is almost impossible to win from the rear at Moonee Valley unless the early pace completely collapses.
*   **Even "Handy" is a Disadvantage:** Similar to Rosehill, even racing in 3rd or 4th position results in a negative return. The advantage is heavily concentrated in the first pair.

## 3. Barrier Draw Bias

**RacingZone Barrier Statistics (Since 2017):**

| Distance | Key Barrier Insights | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint (<=1400m)** | Barrier 1 has a very high 16.4% strike rate. Barriers 1-5 have a clear advantage. | [4] |
| **Mile (1400m-1800m)** | Barrier 1 (19.4%) and Barrier 4 (18.8%) are exceptionally strong. The inside bias is extreme. | [4] |
| **Middle Distance (1800m-2400m)** | Barrier 2 (25.0%) and Barrier 5 (16.7%) are the most successful. | [4] |

**Practical Punting "Valley of Bias" Study:**

This older study found that barriers 7, 8, and 9 were the most favoured, suggesting that being just off the rail but still forward was the ideal position. This contrasts with the more recent RacingZone data. [3]

**Analysis:**

*   **Inside is Everything:** The recent data from RacingZone shows a powerful bias towards the inside barriers. Drawing barrier 1 is a huge advantage, particularly in mile races.
*   **The Short Run to the Turn:** In most starts at Moonee Valley, there is a very short run to the first turn. Any horse drawn wide that doesn't have exceptional gate speed is at high risk of being caught wide and losing significant ground.

## 4. Strategic Implications for TDM

1.  **Leader, Leader, Leader:** The TDM for Moonee Valley must be overwhelmingly focused on identifying the likely leader. This is not just a preference; it is the fundamental principle of racing at this track. The model should apply the strongest possible positive weighting to the likely leader.
2.  **Inside Gates are Paramount:** The model must heavily favour horses drawn in barriers 1-5. A horse with early speed drawn an inside gate is the ideal Moonee Valley profile.
3.  **Penalise Backmarkers Severely:** The model should apply a very strong negative weighting to any horse that is likely to settle further back than midfield. The data shows they have almost no chance of winning.
4.  **Agility Over Stride:** The tight turns favour nimble, agile horses over long-striding gallopers. While difficult to quantify, this is a key qualitative factor to consider in the overlay.
