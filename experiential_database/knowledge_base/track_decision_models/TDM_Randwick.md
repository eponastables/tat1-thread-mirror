**Key Characteristics:**

*   **Long, Rising Straight:** The 410m home straight at Randwick has a noticeable rise, making it a tough test of stamina. Horses must be strong at the end of their races to win here. [1]
*   **Spacious and Fair:** Like Flemington, Randwick is considered a spacious and eminently fair racetrack. The long straight and sweeping turns give every horse a chance. [1]
*   **Multiple Chutes:** Randwick has several starting chutes, which means the start locations and run to the first turn can vary significantly depending on the race distance.

## 3. Barrier Draw Bias

**RacingZone Barrier Statistics (Since 2017):**

| Distance | Key Barrier Insights | Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint (<=1400m)** | Barriers 2, 3, and 4 are very strong (9.9%, 11.8%, 11.7%). There is a clear advantage to drawing an inside gate in sprint races. | [3] |
| **Mile (1400m-1800m)** | Barrier 2 is exceptionally strong (15.8%). Barriers 3 and 4 are also very good. The inside bias is pronounced. | [3] |
| **Middle Distance (1800m-2400m)** | Barrier 1 has a massive 21.1% strike rate. This is a very significant statistic for staying races at Randwick. | [3] |

**Analysis:**

*   **Pronounced Inside Bias:** Across all distances, Randwick shows a strong and consistent bias towards inside barriers. This is one of the most important factors to consider.
*   **The Chutes:** The various starting positions mean that for some starts (e.g., the 1600m "mile" start), there is a long run to the first turn, which can mitigate a wide draw. However, for other starts, the run is much shorter, making an inside draw crucial.

---

## 4. Strategic Implications for TDM

1.  **Inside Gates are Gold:** The TDM for Randwick must have a very strong positive weighting for inside barriers (1-4), particularly in mile and middle-distance races. The 21.1% strike rate for barrier 1 in staying races is a critical piece of data.
2.  **Stamina is Key:** The long, rising straight places a premium on stamina. When assessing horses, look for those with a strong finishing record and proven ability to run out a strong final 400m.
3.  **Assume a General On-Pace Advantage:** While not as pronounced as at Rosehill, the general statistics still favour horses racing near the lead. Apply a moderate positive weighting for on-pace runners.
4.  **Consider the Start Location:** For each race, the model should be aware of the start location and the length of the run to the first turn. A wide barrier is less of a disadvantage from a start with a long run to the turn.
