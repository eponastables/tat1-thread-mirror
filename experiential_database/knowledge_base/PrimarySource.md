## Secondary Sources and Practitioner Explanations

### Comprehensive Methodology Series

*   **Brisnet.com / Frandsen Publishing. "Pace Handicapping with Brohamer Figures." ALL-Ways Software Documentation Series.**

    This four-part series provides detailed, practical explanations of the Brohamer/Sartin methodology, including:

    *   **Part 1: "Velocity-Based Pace Figures"**
        *   Fraction distances for sprints vs. routes
        *   Feet-per-second calculations
        *   Compound ratings formulas (EP, SP, AP, FX)
        *   Complete worked example for a six-furlong race

    *   **Part 2: "Brohamer Track Decision Model"**
        *   Example Decision Model for one-mile dirt routes at Calder Race Course
        *   Methodology for building and maintaining track-specific models

    *   **Part 3: "Percent Early Energy Distribution"**
        *   Formula: `Total Energy = EP + Third Fraction`; `%Early = EP / Total Energy`
        *   Sample %Early zones for Calder one-mile dirt routes

    *   **Part 4: "Turn-Time: The Hidden Fraction"**
        *   Analysis of the second fraction as the critical indicator
        *   Integration with other Brohamer figures

### Software Implementation Documentation

*   **ALL-Ways Handicapping Software. "Brohamer Plus Report." Frandsen Publishing.**

    The ALL-Ways software received explicit permission from both Howard Sartin and Tom Brohamer to include their methodology. The "Brohamer Plus Report" automatically calculates all Brohamer pace figures, maintains Track Decision Models, and presents %Early Energy Distribution Models for every race type.

### Professional Context and Legacy

*   **The Jockey Club. "TrackMaster Introduces Brohamer’s MPH Pro – New Web-Based Handicapping Program." News Release, 5 September 2014.**

    This press release documents the collaboration between Tom Brohamer and TrackMaster. Key information includes:

    *   MPH Pro is a web-based update of the strategies in *Modern Pace Handicapping*.
    *   Features include Rankings grids, Velocity grids, and Projections grids.
    *   Tom Brohamer is quoted: "The new pro version has all the original functionality as well as many new features. And now that it is web based, anyone with a computer, tablet, or smartphone can use it."
    *   Brohamer

    *   Brohamer's credentials: Professional handicapper for over 40 years, featured speaker at handicapping expos, co-led weekly handicapping seminars at Del Mar and Santa Anita with Jim Quinn.

## Related Influential Works

For readers seeking broader context, the following works are frequently mentioned in connection with Brohamer's methodology:

*   **Quinn, Jim. *The Best of Thoroughbred Handicapping*. New York: Daily Racing Form Press, 2002.** (Quinn co-led handicapping seminars with Brohamer at Del Mar and Santa Anita).
*   **Sartin, Howard G., et al. *The Psychology of Handicapping: A Sartin Anthology*.** (Various publications, out of print; Brohamer developed his methodology from the foundational work of Dr. Howard Sartin).
*   **Beyer, Andrew. *Picking Winners: A Horseplayer's Guide*. Boston: Houghton Mifflin, 1975.** (The classic work on speed figures that complements pace-based approaches).
